Connecting Your Tracker

A visual guide to bridging the gap between your Google Sheet student data and AI-powered analysis in NotebookLM.

Preparation

Structure Your Evidence

Clean Your Google Sheet

NotebookLM parses data hierarchically. For the most accurate analysis, ensure your top row contains explicit, descriptive headers like Student Name, Q1: Fractions, and Total Score.

Teacher Tip

Avoid merged cells or "blank" header columns. A flat, simple table allows the AI to map every mark to the correct student instantly.

Launch

Create the Workspace

Initiate a New Notebook

Navigate to notebooklm.google.com. Locate and click the large New Notebook button to create a dedicated analysis environment.

Integration

Import Student Data

Sync with Google Drive

Once the source menu opens, follow this sequence to connect your live data:

Select the Google Drive icon from the list.
Search for your Assessment Tracker spreadsheet.
Select the file and click Insert.
Analysis

Begin the Diagnosis

Query the Evidence

Your sheet will appear in the sidebar under "Sources." The AI is now "grounded" in your class data and ready for questioning.

Initial Diagnostic Query

"Review the data. Which students show high scores in Fractions but a significant drop in Decimal Place Value? Group them for an intervention."
