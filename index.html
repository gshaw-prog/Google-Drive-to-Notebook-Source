Connecting Your Tracker

Syncing Google Sheets with NotebookLM

Preparation

Structure Your Evidence

Clean Your Google Sheet

NotebookLM parses data hierarchically. For the most accurate analysis, ensure your top row contains explicit, descriptive headers like Student Name and Q1: Fractions.

Teacher Tip: Avoid merged cells or blank header columns. A flat, simple table allows the AI to map every mark to the correct student instantly.

Launch

Create the Workspace

Initiate a New Notebook

Navigate to notebooklm.google.com. Click the New Notebook button to create a dedicated analysis environment for your class.

Integration

Import Student Data

Sync with Google Drive

Once the source menu opens, follow this sequence to connect your live data:

Select the Google Drive icon.
Search for your Assessment Tracker spreadsheet.
Select the file and click Insert.
Analysis

Begin the Diagnosis

Query the Evidence

Your sheet will appear in the sidebar under "Sources." The AI is now "grounded" in your class data and ready for questioning.

"Summarise the performance of the class. Review the data and identify which students show high scores in Fractions but a significant drop in Decimal Place Value. Group them for an intervention."
Note: NotebookLM will not change your original Google Sheet; it only reads the data to provide these insights.
